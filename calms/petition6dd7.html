Submitted Petition Text:
Replace ¶¶ 268, 269, 629.6 & 662 with the following material as follows:
  
268
1. A candidate may be recognized as a member of the Society of Saint Barnabas by the district or conference committee on Saint Barnabas Ministries after the candidate has:
       a) Made application in writing to the appropriate committee and has been recommended by the pastor and the church council or the charge conference of the local church in which he or she holds membership.
       b) Completed the Core Curriculum Course for members of the Society of Saint Barnabas.   This core curriculum shall contain modules on the story of Saint Barnabas from the Book of Acts; a review of the history of the office of Exhorter and Lay Speaker; an overview of the vast lay ministries available in the church and community.  It will include modules on the Spiritual Disciplines and on servant ministry.
       c) Had his or her qualifications reviewed and approved by the appropriate committee (see ¶ 258.2 f [7]).
       d) Once all of the above has been completed the Society of St. Barnabas member will be issued a Certificate of Membership in the Society of Saint Barnabas and they may place the initials SSB at the end of his or her name (i.e.  Joann Smith, SSB)            
    2. The member of the Society of Saint Barnabas shall serve the local church in the witness of the spoken word, vital leadership service, and care-giving ministry (see ¶ 266.1). In addition, the SSB member may serve in the district and conference and in local churches other than the local church in which membership is held.    
    3. Recognition as a member of the Society of Saint Barnabas shall be renewed annually by the district or conference committee on Saint Barnabas Ministries after the SSB member has:
       a) Requested in writing the renewal of certification.
       b) Submitted an annual report to the charge conference and the committee on Saint Barnabas Ministries, giving evidence of the satisfactory performance of SSB service.
       c) Been recommended for renewal by the pastor and the church council or charge conference.
       d) Completed an advanced course for members of the Society of Saint Barnabas at least once in every three years.
    4.  All persons currently holding valid credentials as either Local Church Lay Speakers or Certified Lay Speakers will automatically become members of the Society of Saint Barnabas.
Para 269.
A member of the Society of Saint Barnabas who moves may transfer certification to the new district upon receipt of a letter from the previous district’s committee on Saint Barnabas Ministries confirming current certification and the date of completion of the most recent advanced course taken. Further renewal of certification is in accordance with ¶ 268.
Para 629.6.
    a) Every annual conference is encouraged to create a conference committee on Saint Barnabas Ministries or other equivalent structure to fulfill the requirements of ¶¶ 266-268 and to relate to the conference board of laity and the General Board of Discipleship as per ¶ 1115 and others that might apply.
    b) The purpose of a conference committee on Saint Barnabas Ministries is to set criteria and guidelines for district committees on Saint Barnabas Ministries, to develop Society of Saint Barnabas courses and approve courses developed by district committees, and to organize conference-wide  of  Saint Barnabas events.
    c) A conference committee on Saint Barnabas Ministries will consist, at a minimum, of the district directors of Saint Barnabas Ministries or their equivalent.
    d) There will be a conference director of Saint Barnabas Ministries. This position will be filled in a manner to be determined by the annual conference. The conference director will chair the committee. Other officers will be elected by the committee as the committee deems necessary.
Para 662.
Districts are encouraged to create a district committee on Saint Barnabas Ministries related to the annual conference through the conference committee on Saint Barnabas ministries.
    1. The purpose of the district committee on Saint Barnabas Ministries is to plan and supervise the program within the district.
    2. The committee is chaired by the district director of Saint Barnabas Ministries. In addition to the director, membership of the committee will include the district lay leader, the district superintendent, and an instructor of Saint Barnabas courses. Other resource people may be added as needed.
    3. The responsibilities of a district committee on Saint Barnabas Ministries are to provide basic training for members of the Society of Saint Barnabas as recommended by the General Board of Discipleship, or as approved by the conference committee on Saint Barnabas Ministries; to decide who will be recognized as members of the Society of Saint Barnabas; to help match Society of Saint Barnabas members with service opportunities; and to support and affirm SSB members as they serve.
    4. The district committee shall plan advanced courses  that will enable SSB members to maintain that recognition.
   5. The district committee will report to the pastor and charge conference of each of the SSB courses that have been satisfactorily completed by the SSB member.

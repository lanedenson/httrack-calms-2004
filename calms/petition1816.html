Submitted Petition Text:
Amend ¶507 by adding a new number 4 and renumbering the existing material:
Any clergy member, lay member, congregation, or other organization of the United Methodist Church petitioning the General Conference must also provide the following information:
      a)            For individuals: the local United Methodist Church and annual conference in which their membership is held along with identifying data about any offices or positions (paid or volunteer) held within the United Methodist Church and any positions beyond the United Methodist Church that relate to the subject of the petition.  Each petition submitted shall provide evidence of support for the petition in the form of at least 10 but not more than 20 signatures of United Methodists.  Each signatory/ supporter shall provide the same information as the petition initiator re: local church/ annual conference membership, UMC offices and/ or positions, and/or positions beyond the UMC that relate to the petition’s subject.  Signatory/ supporters list shall attempt to reflect evidence of knowledge of the petition’s topic from within the church-at-large.
              b)         For congregations, the petition shall specify the name of the local church along with the names of the district, annual conference to which the church belongs.  One person (clergy or laity) shall sign the petition on behalf of the congregation; that person will provide the same information as required of individuals in 4a above.  In addition, each petition shall provide evidence of support for the petition in the form of at least 10 but not more than 20 signatures of United Methodists, Signatories/ supporters shall meet the same requirements for identifying data as set forth in 4a above and include in the signatories of the church officials designated by the church’s charge conference to officially represent the church (otherwise petitioner must file as an individual).  Signatory/ supporters list shall attempt to reflect evidence of knowledge of the petition’s topic from within the church-at-large.
c)         For other organizations or groups, the petition shall specify the groups name, its raison d’etre or underlying organizational motivation, its officers (when applicable) and its affiliation(s) with a local church, cluster, district and/or annual conference.  One of the organizations members may sign the petition on behalf of the group; if the member holds an office in the organization, the office shall be identified.  The member will provide other identifying data as required of individuals in 4a above.  In addition, each petition submitted on behalf of a group shall provide evidence of support for the petition in the form of at least 10 but no more than 20 signatures of United Methodists.  Signatories/supporters shall meet the same requirements for identifying data as set forth in 4a above with the exception that at least 25% but no more than 50% of the supporting signatures must be from UMC members who do not belong to the organization reflecting evidence of knowledge of the petition’s topic beyond the organization and from within the church-at-large. Each of said signatories shall also provide the same types of identifying data as set forth in 4a above.  Official evidence of a vote of support from an Annual Conference may be substituted in this case for the United Methodist signatories/supporters reflecting evidence of knowledge of the petition’s topic within the church-at-large.    
        
Submitted ByRose Ann Briotte
Knoxville TN

Submitted Petition Text:
II. The UMMIM Network Advisory Committee Structure
      1.  The UMMIM network shall elect an Advisory Committee.  The Advisory Committee shall elect their own officers; establish Mental Illness Ministry goals for the quadrennium which may be applicable for the Church - at- large, the Annual Conference; and the local congregation.  
2.  The Advisory Committee shall give leadership to the online discussion group of United Methodist Mental Illness Ministries network, and educational teams with other church leadership to consult and act on the need for greater understanding of the similarities and differences between ministries to persons with mental and physical disabilities and mental illness in congregations.
3.  The Advisory Committee shall appoint individual Methvision members to tasks in keeping with their gifts and abilities and the needs of the network which will build the network within the United Methodist Church including archives and history, a quarterly online UMMIM Newsletter, promotion, and publicity.  They shall appoint a Methvision task force to provide a listing of Mental Illness/ Mental Health resources for Church leadership who want to provide support to pastors to “lead their congregations by teaching and example”  in ministries which welcome people with mental and physical disabilities and mental illnesses.  ("The Ministry of the Ordained” page 217 ¶331)
4. All meetings shall be planned by the Advisory Committee in consultation with the GBCS contact person.  An initial meeting or the General Meeting shall take place no later than May of the first year of the quadrennium, 2005.  
III. UMMIM Network General Meeting Tasks
A General Meeting shall be called by the members of The Mental Illness Ministries network in consultation with the person assigned to mental illness issues at the GBCS to:
  • Create a “theology of mental and physical disabilities and mental illnesses” based on biblical teachings of inclusiveness, liberation, and hope and the local church’s mission through consultation with theologians and professionals.
    • Discuss the biblical, ethical, and social implications of the existence of/ lack of ministry to persons with mental and physical disabilities and mental illnesses in the church as a whole;  

    • The resulting theology of Mental Illness and the biblical, ethical, and social implications of the existence of / lack of ministry to persons with mental and physical disabilities and mental illnesses will be shared as widely as possible through the educational component of this petition to local congregations; Districts and Conferences; and General Boards and Agencies.  

    • Make Plans to invite other General Boards and Agencies for collaboration with The Mental Illness Ministries network Advisory Committee when the Board or Agencies area of responsibility and/or focus overlaps at times with the work of the Mental Illness Ministries network.  
      
        IV. UMMIM Advisory Committee Collaboration with  
    General Boards and Agencies and Episcopacy  
      
  • Boards, Agencies and groups are asked to describe their mandates and ministries in a brief session to determine how their work under the Administrative Order established by the General Conference is connectional and relates to Caring Communities and ministries to persons with a mental illness, and how all persons with mental and physical disabilities and mental illnesses can and should be able to feel welcome to participate in the full life of the congregation and find friendship and support there.
    • The brief session will be followed by an online seminar in which General Boards and Agencies shall continue to collaborate with the UMMIM network on how mutual support and assistance of the various groups can be developed to accomplish our individual mandates and eliminate duplication of efforts.  
    • In consultation with representatives of the appointive process, the UMMIM network  Advisory Committee shall additionally seek to be educated about the United Methodist Appointive System to be able to assist the Church in educating annual conferences and consult with local congregations upon request, as to how they can give the support and help from the Church when congregations have a pastor who has a mental illness or a member of the pastors family suffers with a mental illness.  

    • The General Board of Ordained Ministry and the Episcopacy shall participate and give direction as they see fit to discussions of preserving and protecting the mental health of our ordained ministers and the mental health of their families and will be asked to give guidance and support to the UMMIM network as it seeks to do its work.
        VI.  Accountability
    The Mental Illness Network and the Advisory Committee shall, in the future as in the past, be directly accountable to the General Board of Church and Society during the 2005-2008 Quadrennium.   The Advisory Committee shall consist of approximately 5-8 persons from the Mental Illness Network and approximately 3-4 persons representing other General Boards and Agencies task forces and groups who attended the General meeting and whose mandated concerns overlap with or relate to the concerns of the Mental Illness Network.  Participants from other General Boards and Agencies must be willing to be a member of an educational team during the deployment phase of this plan, which will provide leadership for the expansion of ministry to persons with a mental illness in the connectional church.
    VI. Training Caring Communities Education Teams
    The Training Meeting shall consist of The Advisory Committee, and General Meeting attendees who volunteer to become a member of a Caring Community Education Team, and others as the budget allows.  They shall work together to form Caring Communities Program Education teams of two or three persons to respond to requests from groups at all levels of the Church who are willing to dialogue about mental illness.  Objectives of the training meeting shall include:  
    1. to develop and model to other participants, styles of program participation, educational techniques and advocacy skills to assist congregations in dispelling myths and fears about mental illness, and reach out to persons with care and concern.  
    2. to create a model of friendship “evangelism” and pastoral care by local congregations for persons who suffer with a mental or physical disability or mental illness.
    3. to experience consultants from a variety of disciplines including disability, theology, homiletics, pastoral care, as well as mental illness advocacy groups who inspire the participants spiritually and will infuse the group with new ideas and challenges for integration of persons with mental illness and mental and physical disabilities into congregational life and ministry.
    Other Training participants who come at their own expense must be willing to serve on a Caring Communities Education Team or be nominated to serve as an active and participating member of the Advisory Committee during the quadrennium.  
    VII. Deployment of Caring Communities Educational Teams
    The Advisory Committee shall request that The Episcopacy, selected General Boards and Agencies, seminaries, and Annual Conferences provide agenda time at an annual meeting, faculty meeting, or other cabinet and conference leadership meeting for the 2005-2008 quadrennium for dialogue with a Caring Communities Education Team as funds allow.
    The Advisory Committee shall coordinate deployment of educational teams from the training meeting based on the responses from invitations requesting that a Caring Communities Education Team be invited for dialogue, consultation, and education.

Amend 266.
    266.  Lay Speaking-1.  A lay speaker  (local church or certified) is a member of a local church or charge who is ready and desirous to serve the Church and who is well informed on and committed to the Scriptures and the doctrine, heritage, organization, and life of The United Methodist Church and who has received  specific training to develop skills in witnessing to the Christian faith through spoken communication, church and community leadership, and care-giving ministries.  The member shall have applied for lay speaking status and been recognized as a lay speaker by the district superintendent through the appropriate conference or district committee on lay speaking ministries.  An applicant for lay speaker recognition  must be active in the support of the local church or charge.  A lay speaker is a lay officer of the Church, who is accountable for his/her ministry to (first) the pastor of the church or charge, and (second, particularly if engaged in ministry beyond the local church or charge) to the district superintendent responsible for overseeing the church or charge.  
   2.  Lay speakers are to serve the local church or charge ( or, if certified or advanced, beyond the local church or charge ) in any way in which their witness or leadership and service inspires the laity to deeper commitment to Christ and more effective discipleship, including the interpretation of the Scriptures, doctrine, organization, and ministries of the church.  This does not, however, prohibit local church lay speakers from holding, in their role as laity, elected or appointed committee or task force positions beyond the local church.
   3.  Through ongoing and  continued study and training a lay speakers should prepare to,  and  then undertake, one or more of the following functions, giving primary attention to service within the local church or charge.
         a)   To  Tt ake initiative in giving leadership, assistance, and support to the program emphases of the church.
         b)   To  Ll ead meetings for prayer, training, study, and discussion when requested by the pastor, district superintendent, or committee on lay speaking.
         c)   To  Cc onduct, or assist in conducting, services of worship, and present sermons and addresses when requested by the pastor, district superintendent, or committee(s) on lay speaking.
         d)   To  Rr elate to appropriate committees and ministry areas in providing leadership for congregational and community life and fostering care-giving ministries.
    4.  Lay speaker training courses shall be those recommended by the General Board of Discipleship, or alternates approved by the conference committee on lay speaking; such alternates should include activities or functions in which, by participating, lay speakers would increase their knowledge, competencies, and skill.  Such tTraining should enable ministries with all language and cultural groups as appropriate.  Lay speaker training shall enable serving the local church and beyond, depending on the level of lay speaker, in the witness of the spoken word, vital leadership service, and care-giving ministry (see 266.1).
  5.  There are three levels of lay speaking, each having different training requirements (see 267-269).  They are local church lay speaker, certified lay speaker, and advanced lay speaker.  Local church status allows being identified as a lay speaker when engaged in activity only at the local church level; certified and advanced lay speakers may be identified as engaged in lay speaker activity at the district or conference levels and in local churches other than the one in which membership is held.
  6.  A candidate may be recognized as a lay speaker at any level by the appropriate district or conference committee after the candidate has:
         a)  Submitted an annual report of ministry activity in which the candidate engaged during the year prior to applying for recognition, including evidence of having completed necessary training plus any additional educational activity;
         b)  Been recommended for recognition by the pastor and the church council or the charge conference of the local church in which membership is held (see 258.1a[1-2]).  The district superintendent or elder presiding at the charge conference, or the pastor in the case of a church council, in cooperation with the secretary of those bodies, shall be responsible for attesting a recommendation;
         c)  Made application for recognition in writing to the District Superintendent through the appropriate district or conference committee;
         d)  Completed the training necessary for recognition at the lay speaking level for which application is being made;
         e)  Had his/her qualifications reviewed and approved by the appropriate district or conference committee; and
         f)  Received evidence in writing, through the appropriate district or conference committee, that the District Superintendent has approved recognition.
    7.  To maintain status, reapplication must be done annually on or about the anniversary date of the previous recognition.  The lay speaker is responsible for notifying the proper committee(s) of the local church well in advance of the anniversary date.  (see 258.1a[1-2])

  Insert new 269
            269.  Advanced Lay Speaker-1.  A candidate may be recognized as an advanced lay speaker after the candidate has completed the basic training course, met the requirements of  266.6, and completed all advanced training.  Advanced training shall consist of:
a)  Four foundational advanced lay speaking courses, as defined by the General Board of Discipleship, and satisfactorily passing the test for each course.  The courses need not be taken in any particular sequence, but Lay Speakers are advised to consult with their pastors in making choices.
b)  One elective advanced training course, selected from the list of courses approved by the General Board of Discipleship (again, lay speakers are advised to consult with their pastors in making this choice), and satisfactorily passing its test.  The five advanced courses must have been completed within five years of the date of taking the first advanced course.
       2.  To maintain status, once every two years dating from the completion of the last advanced course, the advanced lay speaker must take, and satisfactorily pass the test of, at least one advanced course or have engaged in an approved function or activity.
    3.  An advanced lay speaker may pastorally serve a local church or charge as a district superintendent appointee (DSA), either part time or full time, with his/her membership being transferred to that local church or charge.  Should a lay person be appointed as a DSA who does not hold advanced lay speaker status, the appointment shall be conditional upon the schedule the district superintendent requires of the appointee to attain that status.
        a)  The district superintendent shall ensure that all parties, at every level of the Church, are aware that the DSA is laity, not clergy.
         b)  The appointee shall receive remuneration only if such has been negotiated with the local church or charge by the district superintendent.
         c)  The appointee shall not receive conference benefits; however, other benefits under the conference guidelines for lay employees (should such exist and apply) may be provided by the local church or charge in consultation with the district superintendent.
         d)  The appointee may serve as the lay member of annual conference of the local church or charge only if properly elected to that position by the local church or charge.  The appointee shall excuse her- or him-self from serving as chair of the committee on lay leadership, or such other committee responsible for nominations, when candidates for lay member are discussed.

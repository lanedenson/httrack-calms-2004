Amend Section 5.17 of MPP as follows:
  
    5.17    Unclaimed Benefit.  The Administrator may prescribe uniform and nondiscriminatory rules for carrying out the following provisions:
        a.    If a portion (or all) of an Account remains to be distributed to a Participant, Former Participant, or Beneficiary at a time when it is due under the Plan (including, but not limited to, the “required beginning date,” as defined in section 401(a)(9) of the Code) and the Administrator is then unable to locate such person, the Administrator will send notice of such benefit due by a certified letter with return receipt requested to the last known address of such person. If such person fails to contact the Administrator within 12 months (except as provided in Section 5.17b), such benefit will be forfeited (except as provided in Section 5.17c) and will become the benefit of, in the case of a Participant, Former Participant, or alternate payee (within the meaning of Section 10.04), such person’s Beneficiary, or, in the case of a Beneficiary, the Participant’s, Former Participant’s, or alternate payee’s successor Beneficiary (including any default Beneficiaries provided under the terms of the Plan). The Administrator will then send notice by certified letter as provided above to the Beneficiary or successor Beneficiary (including a default Beneficiary), and the process specified above will be repeated until the last successor Beneficiary is sent a notification.
b.    If the last successor or default Beneficiary fails to contact the Administrator within 12 months after being sent notification of a benefit due as provided in Section 5.17a, then the amount specified in Section 5.17a will be forfeited. The Administrator will use such forfeitures to defray the expenses of administering the Plan.db
c.    If, at any time before the expiration of the 12-month period described in Section 5.17b, a Participant, Former Participant, or Beneficiary who is or was due a benefit described in Section 5.17a claims the benefit, the benefit will be paid to such person (notwithstanding any previous forfeiture) if it has not previously been paid to another Participant, Former Participant, or Beneficiary. If the 12-month period described in Section 5.17b has elapsed, then such benefit will be permanently forfeited and used by the Administrator as described in Section 5.17b.
a.    If a Participant fails to properly claim a benefit due hereunder prior to the “required beginning date,” as defined in Section 5.05e(2) and there is no current mailing address on file with the Administrator, the Administrator shall send a certified letter with return receipt requested to the last known address of the Participant indicating that the Participant has 60 days to claim such benefit. If the Participant fails to claim the benefit within the 60-day period, the Participant shall be deemed, in accordance with rules and regulations adopted by the Administrator, to have relinquished any benefit that may be payable to the Participant.
b.    The failure of a Beneficiary to properly claim a benefit due hereunder during the stated time period, or if no time period is stated, then within two years of being eligible to receive the benefit, shall cause the benefit to be considered to have been refused and forfeited and shall cause the benefit to be paid to the secondary Beneficiary or default Beneficiary in accordance with the Plan. If the last default Beneficiary does not claim the benefit within a two-year period commencing with the date on which he/she became eligible to receive the benefit, the benefit shall be considered to be refused and forfeited by said Beneficiary. After the last two-year period has expired, the Administrator shall send a certified letter with return receipt requested to the last known address of the last default Beneficiary indicating that the Beneficiary has 60 days to claim such benefit. Failure to claim the benefit within the 60-day time period shall cause the benefit to be forfeited. Such forfeited amounts shall be added to the reserves of the Plan. However, any such forfeited amount will be reinstated and become payable if a claim is made by the estate of the Participant or Beneficiary. The Administrator shall prescribe uniform and nondiscriminatory rules for carrying out this provision.
        
Amend Section 5.18 of MPP as follows:
  
    5.18    [Reserved] Forfeitures. Notwithstanding Section 4.02, a Participant, Former Participant, or Beneficiary may forfeit an otherwise Vested Account in the following circumstances:
        a.    Missing Participant.  The Accounts of a Participant, Former Participant, or Beneficiary who cannot be located will be handled as described in Section 5.17.
b.    Uncashed Check.  Any Participant, Former Participant, or Beneficiary who has been issued a check for benefits due but who does not return or cash the check within a reasonable period established by the Administrator, after such reasonable notice (or in the case of de minimus amounts, no notice) as the Administrator may determine, will forfeit such benefits. Such forfeited amounts will be used by the Administrator to defray the administrative expenses of the Plan. Uncashed checks returned to the Administrator because the payee is missing or for other reasons are not covered by this Section 5.18b.
c.    Relinquished Benefits.  If a Participant relinquishes a benefit as provided in Section 5.11, it is forfeited. The relinquished benefit will be handled as described in Section 5.11.
d.    Ineligible Clergyperson.  Benefits credited to an ineligible Clergyperson will be forfeited and handled as described in Sections 3.06 and 10.03b.
e.    Election Not to Participate.  Clergypersons who elect not to participate in the Plan under Section 3.07 will be handled as described in that Section, but contributions not received during periods when the election was in force will not be earned or will be permanently forfeited.
        
Amend Section 8.01 of MPP as follows:
  
    8.01    Amendment of the Plan.
  
        a.    General Conference.  The General Conference may amend prospectively or retroactively any or all provisions of this Plan at any time by written instrument identified as an amendment of the Plan effective as of a specified date.  However,
b.    Administrator.  The Board of Directors of the Administrator is authorized to amend any or all provisions of this Plan at any time by resolution, effective as of a specified date: such written instrument in order  
            (1)   to conform the Plan to any applicable law and/or regulations promulgated thereunder; .
(2)    to conform the Plan to The Book of Discipline or changes therein; and
(3)    to take advantage of any permissive law and/or regulations promulgated thereunder, provided that such amendment results in no additional substantial long-term costs to the Plan Sponsors.
              c.    Limitations.  Notwithstanding the foregoing, no amendment to the Plan may decrease the balance in a Participant’s, Former Participant’s, or Beneficiary’s Account. Furthermore, no amendment to the Plan may have the effect of decreasing a Participant’s, Former Participant’s, or Beneficiary’s Vested interest in his or her Account.

        a.    If the Plan Sponsor is one described in Section 2.30a, b, or c herein, the current Adoption Agreement will remain in force for the next Plan Year.

Add the following new Section 9.05 of MPP following Section 9.04 of MPP:
  
    9.05    If a Plan Sponsor is described in Sections 2.30d, e, or f above and it is adopting the Plan with respect to Participants whose participation in the Plan is not required, then the Plan Sponsor may later terminate its Adoption Agreement, or the Administrator may later terminate the Adoption Agreement for the breach of any duty described in Section 7.03 or in the Adoption Agreement, subject to the following requirements:
        a.    Such a Plan Sponsor may terminate its participation in the Plan upon written notice to the Administrator 90 days in advance of the date of such termination. In such a case, the Plan will continue in operation as to all non-terminating Plan Sponsors. As a condition precedent to its right to terminate participation in the Plan, a Plan Sponsor must provide written notice of its intent to its Participants 30 days in advance of such written notice to the Administrator, and must provide to the Administrator evidence of such written notice to the affected Participants.
b.    The Administrator may terminate such a Plan Sponsor’s participation in the Plan for breach of the Plan’s provisions or for late remittance of contributions to the Administrator upon written notice to the Plan Sponsor 90 days in advance of the date of such termination. The Plan Sponsor will have the right to correct such noncompliance and continue participation in the Plan if the correction process is completed no later than 30 days before the effective date of termination. In the case of such a termination, the Administrator will provide notice of the termination to affected Participants.
c.    If at any time after adopting the Plan, an entity ceases to satisfy the requirements or definition of a Plan Sponsor, the entity's Adoption Agreement will terminate, effective as of the date the entity ceases to constitute a Plan Sponsor, or on such later date as the Administrator, in its sole discretion, may prescribe.
            If participation in the Plan by a Plan Sponsor is terminated, the Accounts of the affected Participants, Former Participants, and Beneficiaries related to that Plan Sponsor will remain with the Trustee unless they are transferred into another plan in accordance with the rollover provisions of the Plan or the provisions of law. Each such affected person will have a 100% Vested interest in his or her Account in accordance with the terms of the Plan as then in effect. The former participating Plan Sponsor will provide timely notice to the Administrator concerning any such person’s eligibility to receive benefits under the terms of the Plan. The Trustee will make distributions of benefits to such persons in accordance with the terms of the Plan.

Amend the first sentence of Section 10.04 of MPP as follows:
  
The provisions of Section 10.02 notwithstanding, all or part of a Participant’s Vested benefits arising under this Plan, including Supplement One, may be transferred to one or more “alternate payees” on the basis of a “qualified domestic relations order,” as those terms are defined in Code section 414(p), provided that (1) the Participant makes an assignment of benefits pursuant to the order, and the alternate payee accepts said assignment, on the forms provided by the Administrator; ( 2 1) said order was issued by a court having jurisdiction over the Administrator; or ( 3 2) said order was entered by any other court if the Administrator, in its sole discretion, determines that the order is likely to be entered by a court having jurisdiction over the Administrator.

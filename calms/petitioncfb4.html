Submitted Petition Text:
Amend ¶¶ 15.5 & 15.10 as follows:
  
5. To define and fix the powers, duties, and privileges of the episcopacy, to decide at each of its regular quadrennial meetings whether each bishop elected by a jurisdictional conference shall be retained in office until its next regular quadrennial meeting, to adopt a plan for the support of the bishops, to provide a uniform rule for their retirement, and to provide for the discontinuance of a bishop because of inefficiency or unacceptability.25

  10. To fix a uniform basis upon which bishops shall be elected by the jurisdictional conferences, to establish an orderly and efficient procedure for deciding at each of its regular quadrennial meetings - by a vote cast by the secret ballots of the delegates - whether each bishop elected by a jurisdictional conference shall be retained in office until its next regular quadrennial meeting, and to determine the number of bishops that may be elected by central conferences.28

Amend ¶25.2 as follows:
  
2. To elect bishops - subject to the duty and power of the General Conference at its regular quadrennial meetings to decide whether those bishops shall be retained in office - and to cooperate in carrying out such plans for their support as may be determined by the General Conference.
  
Amend ¶44 as follows:
  
¶ 44. Article II.-The bishops shall be elected by the respective jurisdictional and central conferences and consecrated in the historic manner at such time and place as may be fixed by the General Conference for those elected by the jurisdictions and by each central conference for those elected by such central conference. 78   Each bishop elected by a jurisdictional conference, however, shall stand for retention election at each regular quadrennial meeting of the General Conference.  The retention election shall be conducted only by means of a vote cast by secret ballots of the delegates to the General Conference.  The secret ballots in a retention election shall be substantially in the following form:
  
Shall (name of bishop, jurisdictional conference which elected the bishop, and the annual conference or annual conferences which the bishop now serves) be retained in office until the next regular quadrennial meeting of the General Conference?  Yes ______ No ______
  
The ballots shall be uniform in size, color, shape, and appearance so that it will be impossible to determine whether a ballot was cast by a delegate who was a member of the clergy or a lay member, or whether a ballot was cast by a delegate from a particular jurisdictional conference.
Each delegate to the General Conference (at a regular quadrennial meeting of the General Conference) shall be entitled to vote on the retention of all bishops elected by jurisdictional conferences, regardless of whether a particular bishop was elected by the same jurisdictional conference as that within which the delegate then resides.
To be retained in office, a bishop must receive an affirmative vote equal to, or greater than, fifty percent of the votes cast in the bishop's retention election.   If a bishop does not receive the affirmative vote of at least fifty percent of the votes cast in the bishop's retention election, the bishop's term of office shall expire ninety days after the retention election, and he or she shall not again be eligible for the office of bishop until after two additional quadrennial meetings of the General Conference (not including the meeting at which the retention election is held) have occurred.  A bishop who is not retained shall return to the annual conference in which he or she was serving when elected bishop, and he or she shall be appointed to a position in that annual conference.
Retention elections for bishops elected by jurisdictional conferences shall first be held (1) at the regular quadrennial meeting of the General Conference which amends the Constitution to require retention elections, or (2) if such amendment to the Constitution occurs at a special session of the General Conference, at the first regular quadrennial meeting of the General Conference, which occurs after that special session.
  
Amend ¶48 as follows:
  
¶ 48. Article VI.-The bishops, both active and retired, of The Evangelical United Brethren Church and of The Methodist Church at the time union is consummated shall be bishops of The United Methodist Church.
The bishops of The Methodist Church elected by the jurisdictions, the active bishops of The Evangelical United Brethren Church at the time of union, and bishops elected by the jurisdictions of The United Methodist Church shall have life tenure  terms of office that extend from the times of their initial elections until the holding of their retention elections at the first regular quadrennial meeting of the General Conference which occurs after their respective initial elections; thereafter, their terms of office shall be (1) if they are retained in their retention elections, until the holding of their retention elections at the next regular quadrennial meeting of the General Conference, or (2) if they are not retained in their retention elections, until the ninetieth day after their retention elections.  Each bishop elected by a central conference of The Methodist Church shall have such tenure as the central conference electing him shall have determined.84
The jurisdictional conference shall elect a standing committee on episcopacy to consist of one clergy and one lay delegate from each annual conference, on nomination of the annual conference delegation. The committee shall review the work of the bishops, pass on their character and official administration, and report to the jurisdictional conference its findings for such action as the conference may deem appropriate within its constitutional warrant of power. The committee shall recommend the assignments of the bishops to their respective residences for final action by the jurisdictional conference.

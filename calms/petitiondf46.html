GLOBAL EDUCATION FUND

“A Global Church in a Global World”
  
The General Board of Higher Education and Ministry proposes to the 2004 General Conference the creation of a Global Education Fund of $10 million to strengthen United Methodist schools, colleges, universities, and theological schools in the worldwide network. The new fund will enable the Church to recruit, prepare, nurture, and educate Christian leaders - lay and clergy - for the mission of Christ in the world, and to become a global influence for peace and conflict resolution.      
“A sower went out to sow his seed; and some fell into fertile soil and grew, and yielded a hundredfold.” (Luke 8) The United Methodist Church has yielded yet even more than a hundred fold throughout the world as our missionaries have spanned the globe for three centuries, and planted Methodist roots and heritage in fertile soil. It is now a new millennium and opportunities for renewing historic ministries are in our grasp. When determining and prioritizing its mission and ministry, The United Methodist Church must take into serious consideration the demographic changes within Christianity. Today the Christian population of the world is over 2 billion: Several hundred million in Europe, 480 million in Latin America (34%), 360 million in Africa (25%), 313 million in Asia (22%), and 260 million in North America (18%) (Philip Jenkins, “The Next Christianity”). We see a Christian revolution in progress that is rapidly shifting the Christian population from the global North to the global South. For instance, the Christian population growth in Africa is phenomenal. In 1900 Africa had just 10 million Christians (or 9%) of a total population of 107 million. Today, the Christian population in Africa is 360 million (or 46%) of the total population of 784 million. These demographic data are consistent with the United Methodist Church’s membership growth as reported at the April 28-May 2, 2003 Council of Bishops Meeting. The report shows that the denomination’s membership grew beyond the ten million mark for the first time since 1979. That increase was due to growth in numbers outside the United States, particularly in Africa. The report also shows the denomination’s current membership distribution as:

While the continuing mission and ministries of the General Board of Global Ministries in Africa, Asia, Eastern Europe, and Latin America, significantly contribute to the growth of the Methodism in these areas, those fast growing churches are desperately in need of well trained pastors and well educated lay leaders, and access to formal education from elementary education to higher education for a new generation of Christian leaders. It is time for The United Methodist Church to sow new seed to best serve those United Methodists in the global South and North in a uniquely United Methodist way. The developing world-wide network of Methodist institutions training future leaders requires nurturing so that they have not grown up only to wither away.  
The United Methodist Church has been a global church in education since the early 1880’s when The United Methodist Church sent well trained and dedicated missionaries with the goal of  “Spreading the Gospel and Making Disciples for Jesus Christ” (Francis Asbury) through education. They founded educational institutions everywhere. Today, there are 748 Methodist schools, colleges, universities, and theological schools in 69 nations that are joining with Methodist congregations “to give the key of knowledge in a general way to children, and those of the poor in the vicinity of small towns and villages.” Those United Methodist and Methodist educational institutions generated hundreds and thousands of world-class leaders for the church and world who made notable differences in advocating peace and the betterment of people and their lives. Those institutions must do more in educating and developing world-class clergy and lay leaders today. No other Protestant denomination can do more in educational mission and endeavors than The United Methodist Church because of its unique connectional system.  
The Global Education Fund is based on the knowledge and experience gained from Africa University and from visits and consultations with United Methodist and Methodist educational institutions and theological schools around the globe. Direct involvement of educational professionals in The United Methodist Church and the United Methodist-related schools, colleges, universities, and theological schools, will assure the success of the Global Education Fund and its mission and programs, as with Africa University. Bringing together United Methodist professionals under the leadership of the GBHEM staff with moderate seed funds will help to leverage significant local funds and resources sufficient to sustain the programs while strengthening the global connectional system and the common roots from which it sprang.  
  
Global Education Fund
        Purpose:
        The Global Education Fund will be an integral part of the GBHEM Technical Assistance Program, working to improve, strengthen, and advance Methodist-related schools, colleges, universities, theological schools, and training programs for pastors for the purpose of developing “a new generation of clergy and lay leaders who will commit boldly to Jesus Christ and be characterized by intellectual excellence, moral and spiritual courage, and holiness of heart and life.”  (GBHEM Strategic Plan)
The Global Education Fund will create and support the United Methodist Scholars Program of GBHEM, identifying and recruiting qualified United Methodist scholars, and arrange for teaching and administrative assignments at Methodist-related schools, colleges, universities, and theological schools throughout the world on a cost-sharing basis. This program includes advanced and re-tooling training opportunities for administrative and academic staff members of the schools, colleges, universities, and theological schools, as well as clergy and lay leaders of the church through focused seminars and workshops. Receiving educational institutions will be responsible for the scholars= salaries and local living expenses. The Global Education Fund will be responsible for their benefits and travel expenses.

        Fund Amount and Source:
$10 million for the 2005-2008 quadrennium through a general apportionment fund
        
Program Goals:  
Technical Assistance Program:
  
1.  Design and implement a worldwide information and distance education network;
2.  Design and develop an institutional review process to assure institutional viability and quality;
3. Provide technical assistance to individual schools, colleges, universities, and theological schools to improve and strengthen educational programs and delivery for the purpose of developing clergy and lay leaders for the church and society.
United Methodist Scholars Program:
        
1. Support the education of qualified United Methodists for leadership.
a) Identify, recruit, and assign 50 United Methodist scholars (30 in 2005, 40 in 2006, and 50 in 2007);
2. Develop the exchange of scholars in the worldwide network of Methodist institutions of theological and higher education.
b) Identify 25 educational institutions in need of the United Methodist Scholars.

Eligibility Criteria:
1. A member institution of the International Association of Methodist Schools, Colleges, Universities, and Theological Schools (the IAMSCU member institutions are identified by the World Methodist Council member churches as their related educational institutions). Special attention will be given to those educational institutions in the Central Conferences of The United Methodist Church.
2. A new governing, administrative, or program initiative that assists in institutional improvement and advancement for the purpose of developing Methodist lay and clergy leaders
3. Ability to sustain the initiative with sufficient resources (staff and finance)
  
Administration:   
In cooperation with United Methodist-related academic institutions in the United States and GBGM, GBHEM is fully equipped to design and implement both programs -- Technical Assistance Program and United Methodist Scholars Program.  GBHEM would be fully accountable for administration.
GBHEM will use the following process in administering the programs:
  
Technical Assistance Program
1. An eligible institution requests technical assistance for a new initiative that assists the institution with improvement and advancement. The application must have clearly defined goals, programs and costs.  
  
2. A GBHEM committee consisting of three persons from the Division of Higher Education and three persons from the Division of Ordained Ministry (a staff member and a board member from each division) and a staff member of GBGM, reviews the request and takes an appropriate action.
3. The GBHEM committee organizes a team to provide technical assistance that may involve an institutional assessment, conceptualization of the new initiative, program development, resource deployment, and implementation plan development.
4. The team’s technical assistance report and recommendation will be reviewed by the GBHEM committee for start-up funding support.
5. The institution will implement the new program according to the implementation plan and submit progress reports to the GBHEM committee.
6. The GBHEM committee continues to monitor the progress and provides further technical assistance if needed.
    
          United Methodist Scholars Program
1. An eligible institution submits a request and rationale to GBHEM for a United Methodist scholar for either a short-term (6 months), or a long-term (12-24 months) period together with a local funding commitment and clearly defined goals, programs and costs.
2. The GBHEM committee reviews the request and takes an appropriate action.
3. The GBHEM committee identifies a candidate and sends his/her qualifications to the institution together with a cost sharing financial package for review and approval.
4. The GBHEM committee continues to monitor the United Methodist scholar’s performance and the institution’s satisfaction.
        
        Evaluation:  
        A quadrennial performance evaluation/review of the Technical Assistance Program and the United Methodist Scholars Program will be conducted by GBHEM. The evaluation/review will be based on the stated goals, programs and costs associated with the requested programs. Special attention will be given to the outcomes of the programs.
